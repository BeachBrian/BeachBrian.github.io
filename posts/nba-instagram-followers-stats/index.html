Are the Indiana Pacers the least popular team on Instagram?

Pacers
NBA
Social Media
Author

Brian Beach

Published

May 10, 2023

Some NBA fans get to spend the month of May watching their team take part in a heated playoff series on national television.

For others, May is a month to spend several hours combing through social media data and writing code to determine which players are underrated and deserve more respect the following year.

Over the last few seasons, fans of the Indiana Pacers have been part of the latter category. And when they’re not busy crying about how one of their rookies wasn’t a Rookie of the Year finalist, many are often seen finding alternative methods to complain about how their players are consistently overlooked and underrated.

One of the most straightforward ways to determine a player’s popularity in the league is the number of Instagram followers they have.

Thanks to a website with the suspiciously straightforward title —popularbasketballers.com — that number is easily accessible for 443 professional basketball players as of April 25, 2023.

Unsurprisingly, LeBron James with his more than 150 million Instagram followers lead the league, with Steph Curry’s 50 million in a distant second place. The third highest follower count belongs to Russell Westbrook with around 22 million, making both LeBron and Steph substantial outliers in the league.

However, even after removing the two most popular players in the league from the dataset, Lakers and Warriors players have the highest number of average Instagram followers of any team. Meanwhile, the Indiana Pacers have the second lowest average in the league, with just over 200,000 followers. That may seem like a lot to a normal person, but that number is significantly low for an NBA player. Only the San Antonio Spurs have a lower average follower count.

library(tidyverse)
library(rvest)
library(hoopR)
library(scales)
library(gt)
library(cluster)
library(ggrepel)

followersurl <- "https://www.popularbasketballers.com/"

followers <- followersurl %>% 
  read_html() %>% 
  html_nodes(xpath = '//*[@id="myTable"]') %>% 
  html_table()

followersdf <- followers[[1]]

logs23 <- hoopR::load_nba_player_box(2023)

season23 <- logs23 %>%  
  filter(minutes>0) %>% 
  group_by(athlete_display_name) %>% 
  summarise(
    AvgPoints = mean(points),
    AvgRebounds = mean(rebounds),
    AvgAssists = mean(assists),
    AvgMinutes = mean(minutes),
    AvgSteals = mean(steals),
    Team = team_name
  )

logsig <- season23 %>% 
  left_join(followersdf, by=c("athlete_display_name" = "Name"))

goodlogsig <- logsig %>% 
  filter(`Instagram Followers`>0)

betterlogsig <- goodlogsig %>% mutate(`Instagram Followers`=gsub(",","",`Instagram Followers`)) %>% 
  mutate(`Instagram Followers` = as.numeric(`Instagram Followers`))

teamsig <- betterlogsig %>%
  group_by(athlete_display_name) %>% 
  summarise(
    PlayerAccounts = n(),
    AvgIgFollowers = mean(`Instagram Followers`),
    TotalIgFollowers = sum(`Instagram Followers`)
  )

avgigbyteam <- betterlogsig %>% 
  group_by(Team) %>% 
  summarise(
    AverageFollowers = mean(`Instagram Followers`)
  ) %>% 
  filter(Team != "Team LeBron" &
         Team != "Team Giannis") %>% 
  arrange(desc(AverageFollowers))

bestlogsig <- betterlogsig %>% 
  filter(athlete_display_name != "LeBron James" &

Player success on the court is linked to high follower counts online, but the link is not nearly as strong as some may expect. In fact, only 22% of the variation in Instagram follower counts can be attributed to a player’s average points per game.

Still, it is clear that Pacer players are far less popular than their peers around the league. Every Pacer who averaged more than 5 points per game in the 2022-2023 season has fewer Instagram followers than the linear trend line would suggest, while stars in larger markets such as Russell Westbrook and Klay Thompson have far more followers than their points per game would predict.

According to the dataset, only James Harden has fewer Instagram followers than Tyrese Haliburton, but upon further inspection, the dataset included the follower count from Harden’s old account from 2017. James Harden’s current account has around 12 million followers, nearly 40 times more than Haliburton’s total.

There are also additional issues with determining a team’s popularity solely from Instagram. For example, two-time league MVP Nikola Jókic does not have an Instagram page at all while Ja Morant recently deactivated his account and is not part of the dataset.

Unlike the NBA playoffs, the results of this analysis are not to be taken too seriously, but it is clear that the Indiana Pacers lack the league-wide popularity other players enjoy, despite producing similar outcomes on the court.
